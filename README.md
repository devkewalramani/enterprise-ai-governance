## Overview

A framework for evaluating, governing, and optimizing enterprise generative AI and agentic AI deployments. Developed from applied advisory work with AI-native companies and enterprise buyers, and informed by MIT's Applied Agentic AI for Organizational Transformation program.

This framework addresses the hardest problem in enterprise AI: not the technology, but the commercial and governance motion. Most enterprises can access capable AI models. Few have the frameworks to evaluate vendors objectively, govern deployments responsibly, or optimize costs without sacrificing performance.

## Core Framework Components

**Vendor Evaluation**  
A weighted scoring model for evaluating AI platform vendors across capability, cost, governance, integration, and support dimensions. Designed for enterprise procurement contexts where multiple stakeholders have competing priorities.

**Deployment Governance**  
A governance structure for production-grade AI deployments covering model selection, prompt management, output monitoring, risk escalation, and continuous improvement cycles.

**Cost Optimization**  
A framework for optimizing AI infrastructure costs across model tiers (frontier, mid-tier, fine-tuned, open source) based on task complexity routing and usage pattern analysis.

**POC Design**  
A structured approach to proof of concept design that defines success criteria, timeline, evaluation methodology, and go/no-go decision frameworks before the POC begins.
